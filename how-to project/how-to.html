HOW TO DESIGN A BRAND IDENTITY FOR A STARTUP IN 3 STEPS

03.02.22 | David Osei

  • 5 min read
  • Brand Identity, Startup, Strategy, Design business

    • A common mistake a lot of freelancers make is to approach brand identity from a one-size fits all angle. Different businesses at different stages have very different needs. Knowing this as a designer and factoring that into your process will have you delivering results that your client’s business needs. I won’t bore you by explaining what brand identity is because you’ve probably read that a thousand times. Let’s jump right in.

    1. KNOW YOUR CLIENT'S BUSINESS NEEDS AND TARGET AUDIENCE.

    This is an important, if not the most important step in the branding process. Set up a meeting to understand the client’s business and needs. For a lot of first time owners,the freelancer needs to guide the conversation by asking questions that reveal the business’ immediate design needs and who their target audience is and how much to price the effort required to execute the branding process.
    At the end of this stage the designer should have;

    • Identified the brand's target audience.
    • Outlined the brand design goals
    • Agreed on the price with the client
    • Agree on the creative brief with the client

    2. ALWAYS START WITH STRATEGY.

    The client may argue that they don’t need it because its an extra expense. Effective brand strategy provides a central, unifying idea around which all behavior, actions, and communications are aligned. A good strategy aligns the brand vision, actions, expression and experience. Define the strategy in collaboration with the business owner through extensive dialogue.
    The deliverables at this stage are;

    • Create user personas for the brand
    • Define the brand's personality and tone of voice
    • Develop the big idea to to guide the brand identity development

    3. DESIGN THE BRAND IDENTITY.

    Once the strategy is defined and agreed upon; designing the brand's identity begins. Remember, design is an iterative process. Involve your client as much as possible and as much as possible do not rush the process.
    “You design for durability, for function, for usefulness, for rightness, for beauty.” -Paul Rand.
    The deliverables to present to the client are;

    • Logomark and signature
    • Color Palette
    • Typography
    • Graphic Elements
    • Brand identity Guidelines
